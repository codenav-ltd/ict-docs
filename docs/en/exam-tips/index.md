# Exam Tips Overview

> What separates a 4 from a 5* is rarely "more knowledge". It is **technique**, **time discipline** and **avoiding self-inflicted wounds**.

## Universal advice (both papers)

1. **Read the question twice.** Underline command verbs (*explain, identify, write, design*).
2. **Plan before you write.** 30 seconds of structure beats 5 minutes of rambling.
3. **Mark allocations are your stopwatch.** A 1-mark sub-question gets 1 minute, not 5.
4. **If you don't know, write what you do know.** Partial marks add up.
5. **Use diagrams.** A clear diagram earns marks faster than a paragraph.

## The 1-2-3 review

After each past paper:

1. **Mark yourself honestly.**
2. **Tag every wrong answer** with module + sub-topic.
3. **Build a personal "weak topic" deck.**

That deck is your single most valuable revision asset in S6.
